About Me

In the field of Software Engineering, I’m a “Work Hard, Play Hard” type of engineer. When I see a need in the workplace, I expand myself to fill it while doing the due diligence of research and training in order to fill that need to the best of my ability. Efficiency is my drive. Productivity is my reward.

When it comes to assignments, I have yet to encounter one I won’t agree to do. There are legacy applications and ugly tasks that many employees will shy away from. I’m the one who elects to learn how to do them. Give me your tired, your poor, your huddled code yearning to breathe free<>, and I will stand up to the challenge.

Team culture is big to me. My success in different industries of sales and customer service has given me a great set of soft skills, a strong aim to please, and goal of delivering the best service I can in whatever I’m delivering. You’ll get someone who is compassionate, positive, motivating, charismatic, understanding, and organic. If you need someone who is energetic with or without the coffee machine, look no further.

I’m big on onboarding new hires and getting people up to speed in an efficient way. Whether it’s creating documents and illustrations, showing them around, doing code reviews, one-on-one guidance, I always make myself available and seek them out for questions as well. Now that I’ve reached mid-level, I have set some goals for myself to start grooming new talent. Being someone who has a passion for learning, I have researched multitudes of learning styles. This has led me to be rather efficient in teaching others, as well as myself.

Continuous growth and education is a priority of mine in software engineering.I push regularly to adapt to new technologies and research trends to stay at the top of the market. Problem solving and overcoming challenges give the best feeling in the world. When I started learning Java in 2014, I finally found something that offered me a challenge. The increasing difficulty of learning more intricacies of a programming language satisfied my hunger for a career that gave me purpose. I finally felt like my mental processors were running. I often felt under-challenged in school and college. Since then, my passion and excitement for the field has only grown.

Whether you’re getting to know me as an artist, software engineer, freelance web developer, student, friend, colleague, mentor/mentee, or business partner, one thing I found out is that I’m pretty hard to forget.

‘Omify’ is a term that came from a previous manager which was used to describe the unique way I made my environment better. I always wanted to make things beautiful and ordered, simplistic and colorful. I didn’t care if it took my own efforts and money. I just wanted to make sure I left my mark on things being better.

When I’m not working or doing artwork, I spend time with my son, Christopher (age 7). He’s got a fantastic journey ahead of him in IT with a great mentor as a mom. He’s also the very reason I got into IT. After being diagnosed with Autism, I wanted to help him become self-sufficient as an adult and I started with him early. I looked over his strengths and one of them clearly lied within the computing field. He was so intuitive and picked up on everything fast. I did some research and found IT to be a great field for people with autism. I switched my major from Architecture & Urban Planning to Information Science and Technology and started preparing to teach him. These days, he’s just a kid now, but I sneak programming concepts into his life via video games and other STEM projects.
